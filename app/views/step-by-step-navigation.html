Get funding as a service provider

Check what you need to do to get your funding.

  1. Step 1 Review guidance, rules and timetables

    Review the ILR data collection timetables for the Academic Year to understand what learner data is needed, and for what purpose.

    The ILR provider support manual, ILR specification documents and funding rules help inform you of what data is needed for each type of learner and includes the codes needed to complete ILR returns.

  2. Step 2 Generate an ILR (Individual Learner Record) file

    Most training providers use a management information (MI) system to record information about their learners. If you do not have one, then use the free learner entry tool for up to 500 learners.

    If you need to combine multiple ILR files, then download the ILR file merge tool to merge multiple files before you upload the data.

  3. Step 3 Validate ILR before submission

    The Funding Information System (FIS) is available for download. This software is supplied free of charge to providers who submit ILR data to the ESFA. You can use FIS to validate ILR files before they are submitted on Submit Learner Data and to produce indicative funding reports.

    You can download FIS from the Tools and Services area on the front page of Submit Learner Data.

  5. Step 4 Submit ILR file

    Upload your ILR file using Submit learner data

    If you are submitting apprenticeships data, make sure the data has an approved employer commitment in the Apprenticeship service.

    Timetable: Data collection maintenance schedule for 2021 to 2022 - GOV.UK (www.gov.uk)

    See important dates (table below)

  6. Step 5 Resolve any data issues and check for accuracy

    You can use our reports to check your ILR data. These reports are available for download on Submit Learner Data after you have submitted an ILR file and in FIS whenever you load an ILR file into the application

    It is important to familiarise yourself with these reports in order to assure yourself that your returns are accurate and have passed validation.

    Key reports to check are:

    1. data validation, for errors or warnings associated with specific learners
    2. provider funding reports, to check that the indicative funding is correct

    If necessary, correct any data errors or issues and upload a newer version of your ILR file.

    Guidance, including support from peers, can be found by using:

    1. ESFA communities (peer support forum)
    2. ESFA help centre
    3. Update the ESFA weekly newsletter which you can subscribe for alerts

    Self-service information

    1. Our information on ESFA: funding rules, rates and formula tells you how we use your data and describes how the allocations process works

    There is a suite of information about ILR data submissions, including how and when to return data.

  7. Step 6 Check data for compliance or audit issues

    We review all submissions after the return deadline and create funding and monitoring reports. You can use these to review and correct any learner data that is reported as not looking credible.

    You can use the provider data self-assessment toolkit (PDSAT) to track data issues that our audit team are monitoring.
